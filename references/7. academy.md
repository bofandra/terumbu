# Terumbu Academy — Detailed UI/UX Plan

The **Academy Page** should become the learning gateway of Terumbu.eco. It must serve both first-time visitors discovering marine conservation and returning users continuing courses connected to campaigns, expeditions, or professional development.

The page should feel like a combination of:

* Coursera for structured learning
* Duolingo for approachable progress
* LinkedIn Learning for verified credentials
* A conservation field-preparation portal
* Terumbu.eco’s Impact Passport ecosystem

The central message:

> **Learn about conservation, apply it in the field, and record your achievements in your Impact Passport.**

---

# 1. Primary Objectives

The Academy page should help users:

* Discover conservation courses
* Continue unfinished learning
* Follow structured learning tracks
* Prepare for expeditions
* Earn verified certificates
* Understand projects they support
* Convert learning into field participation
* Add learning records to their Impact Passport

The ideal user flow is:

```text
Discover a topic
→ Choose a learning track
→ Start a course
→ Complete lessons and assessment
→ Earn certificate
→ Apply learning through projects or expeditions
```

---

# 2. Main User Types

## New learner

Has little conservation knowledge and needs beginner-friendly content.

## Campaign supporter

Wants to understand the ecosystem or project they funded.

## Expedition participant

Must complete preparation and safety modules before departure.

## Student

Wants certificates, structured learning, and practical experience.

## Volunteer

Needs orientation before joining conservation activities.

## Professional

Wants credible sustainability, marine conservation, ESG, or biodiversity credentials.

## Corporate employee

Participates through a company-sponsored learning program or challenge.

# 29. Learning Streak

A streak can support retention but should remain secondary.

Example:

```text
4-week learning streak
```

Healthy rules:

* Any meaningful lesson activity counts
* Users can pause during expeditions or travel
* Do not punish users with alarming messages
* Do not make streaks required for certificates

Use encouragement:

```text
You learned for 35 minutes this week.
```

Not:

```text
Your streak will be destroyed tonight.
```

# 46. Accessibility

Minimum requirements:

* WCAG AA contrast
* Keyboard-accessible search and filters
* Proper course-card heading hierarchy
* Captions and transcripts for videos
* Audio transcripts
* Downloadable text alternatives
* Accessible quizzes
* No timer unless educationally necessary
* Screen-reader-readable progress bars
* Visible focus indicators
* Reduced-motion support
* Touch targets of at least 44 × 44 px
* Course difficulty represented by text, not color alone

Example progress label:

```text
Coral Restoration Fundamentals is 68 percent complete. Four of six modules completed.
```

* Purple for certificates
* Green for completed status
* Soft aqua and sand backgrounds

## Typography

* Strong, friendly headings
* Highly readable lesson information
* Limited uppercase text
* Clear metadata hierarchy

## Imagery

Use a mix of:

* Real conservation photography
* Educational marine illustrations
* Diagrams
* Instructor portraits
* Course interface previews
