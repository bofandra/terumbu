# Next Page: Digital Impact Passport

The next page should be the **Impact Passport**, because it is Terumbu.eco’s strongest long-term differentiator.

It transforms scattered activities—donations, coral sponsorships, expeditions, courses, volunteering, and certificates—into one verified conservation profile that users can keep, share, and grow over time.

The page should feel like a combination of:

* LinkedIn profile
* Travel passport
* Strava activity history
* Digital certificate wallet
* Conservation impact report

---

# 1. Page Purpose

## Primary objective

Give each user a clear, credible record of their conservation journey.

## Secondary objectives

* Encourage continued participation
* Allow users to share verified impact
* Display badges and certificates
* Show personal impact geographically
* Build trust through evidence and methodology
* Support professional, academic, and community recognition
* Enable private, public, and link-only visibility

## Core emotional message

> “This is not just what I donated. This is what I helped make possible.”

---

# 2. Main User Types

## Individual supporter

Wants to see and share donations, coral sponsorships, and campaign participation.

## Expedition participant

Wants a verified record of field activities and travel experience.

## Student or learner

Wants course certificates and conservation-learning achievements.

## Volunteer

Wants documented service hours and verified activities.

## Professional

Wants a credible public profile for ESG, sustainability, or conservation involvement.

## Corporate employee

Wants personal activities connected to the company’s conservation program.

---

# 3. Page Modes

The Impact Passport should support three main modes.

## Private dashboard view

Visible only to the user.

Includes:

* Full donation records
* Receipts
* Private booking information
* Draft profile settings
* Exact activity history
* Privacy controls

## Public profile view

Visible to anyone according to user settings.

Includes only approved information:

* Display name
* Profile photo or initials
* Impact summary
* Selected activities
* Certificates
* Badges
* Approximate impact locations

## Link-only view

Accessible only through a unique link.

Useful for:

* Job applications
* University applications
* Scholarship submissions
* Community membership
* Corporate reporting

# 8. Verification Status

Verification should strengthen credibility without overstating what Terumbu.eco has validated.

## Suggested verification levels

### Identity Verified

The user’s account identity has been confirmed.

### Activity Verified

The activity was recorded through Terumbu.eco or a verified partner.

### Field Participation Verified

Attendance was confirmed by an expedition or NGO partner.

### Certificate Verified

The certificate was issued by Terumbu Academy or an approved organization.

## Verification display

Example:

```text
Verified Impact Passport
12 verified activities
3 verified certificates
```

Tooltip:

```text
Verification confirms that these activities were recorded through Terumbu.eco or an approved partner. It does not represent an independent audit of all environmental outcomes.
```

Since joining Terumbu.eco, Raka has supported seven conservation projects across Raja Ampat, Wakatobi, Bali, and Labuan Bajo. His activities include sponsoring 45 coral fragments, joining three field expeditions, completing 12 conservation courses, and contributing 28 volunteer hours.

* Edit the wording

# 43. Visual Design Direction

## Passport identity

* Deep navy
* Ocean blue
* Gold or warm sand accents
* Subtle coral emblem
* Premium but approachable

## Main content

* White background
* Soft aqua section backgrounds
* Thin neutral borders
* Rounded cards
* Large spacing
* Evidence-first photography

## Status colors

* Green: verified or completed
* Blue: active or informational
* Orange: pending or attention
* Gray: private or inactive
* Red: revoked or invalid

Never depend on color alone.

# 47. Success Metrics

Track:

* Passport activation rate
* Public-profile publishing rate
* Share-link creation rate
* Passport-view rate
* Certificate verification clicks
* PDF export rate
* Profile completion rate
* Activity-verification submissions
* Shared-profile conversion to signup
* Return visits after project updates
* Percentage of users continuing toward a milestone

The Impact Passport succeeds when users see Terumbu.eco as a **long-term identity and record of conservation participation**, rather than a platform they visit only when making a donation.
