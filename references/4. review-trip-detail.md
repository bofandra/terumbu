## Overall Assessment

The Trip Detail page is now a much stronger first-version expedition conversion page. It has a visual gallery, breadcrumb, rich trip summary, rating/participant proof, verified partner summary, quick facts, sticky booking card, participant and departure controls, price breakdown, trust indicators, mobile booking bar with bottom sheet, sticky section navigation, conservation-impact section, impact allocation, associated campaign card, itinerary, dates, inclusions/exclusions, requirements, route map, accommodation, field team, preparation course, sustainability, reviews, trip updates, cancellation policy, FAQ, related trips, final CTA, loading state, and error state.

The implementation now makes the public trip-detail information partner/admin manageable. Expedition detail sections are normalized through `expeditions.metadata`, exposed as editable structured JSON in admin expedition management and the new partner Expeditions workspace, and rendered through `getExpeditionDetail()` with safe defaults for older rows. Departure-level public details such as dates, capacity, status, guide, meeting point, minimum participants, and weather advisory are also manageable by partner admins for expeditions linked to their campaigns.

The implementation remains conservative with the current schema. The content is persisted and manageable, but most reference sections still share one JSON document instead of dedicated normalized tables. The remaining gaps are mainly backend/product depth: media libraries, itinerary/add-on/review tables, waitlist/private-trip workflows, minimum participant status automation, reportable safety/accommodation records, and a full five-step checkout flow.

## Implementation Strengths

- The trip page now explains both the travel product and the conservation program before checkout.
- Booking price and availability are no longer hidden behind checkout.
- The page uses existing PostgreSQL records where available and stores editable first-version trip detail sections in expedition metadata where the schema has not yet been specialized.
- Partner admins now have a focused Expeditions workspace for all public trip-detail information tied to their campaigns.
- The booking backend continues to handle actual persistence: booking, participants, payment, seat updates, email logs, analytics, and dashboard integration.
- Mobile conversion is covered with a persistent booking bar and bottom sheet.

## Remaining Product/Data Enhancements
